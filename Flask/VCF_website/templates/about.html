POPULATION GENOMICS


Population genetics is the study of genetic variation within and between populations and the evolutionary factors that explain this variation. Through the analysis of genetic variation, inferences from population genomic data can be applied to conservation genetics, evolutionary biology and precision medicine, etc.

IGSR

The International Genome Sample Resource collects data from multiple human populations through Variant Call Format (VCF) files, containing genotype/haplotype information for sequenced samples at each called SNP. IGSR provided our team with the usability and accessibility of data from the 1000 Genomes Project.


IGSR website

POPgen

POPgen explores the annotation and description of single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) within five human populations. SNPs are the key controllers of genetic diversity.
